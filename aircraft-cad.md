## Overview

This project focuses on the design and assembly of a multi-component aircraft model using SolidWorks. The goal was to create a complete mechanical system with realistic geometry, emphasizing complex surface modeling and component integration.

The project highlights key challenges in 3D CAD design, including aerodynamic shape construction, multi-part assembly, and geometric consistency across components.

## System Design

The aircraft is composed of multiple subsystems designed and integrated into a full assembly:

- Fuselage (main body structure)  
- Wings and external fuel tanks  
- Propeller and hub system  
- Landing gear (wheel and support structure)  
- Tail system (horizontal and vertical stabilizers)  

Each component was modeled independently and assembled with alignment and geometric constraints.

## Key Techniques

- Loft-based surface modeling with guide curves  
- Spline-based geometry construction  
- Reference plane definition for multi-section design  
- Revolve, pattern, and boolean operations  
- Multi-part assembly and alignment constraints  

---

## Challenges

- Constructing aerodynamic geometries without predefined airfoil templates  
- Managing 3D guide curves for stable loft generation  
- Ensuring alignment across curved and non-planar components  
- Maintaining consistency between independently designed parts  

---

## Key Insights

- Complex CAD modeling requires careful planning of reference geometry  
- Loft operations are highly sensitive to sketch quality and alignment  
- Multi-component systems demand both geometric precision and integration strategy  
- Iterative refinement is essential for achieving consistent assemblies
